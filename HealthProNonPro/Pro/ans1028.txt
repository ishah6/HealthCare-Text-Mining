Redundant colon is a normal anatomical variant of the colon with presence 
of extra colonic loops. It has typically has no direct major health 
consequences but may rarely cause volvulus and lead to intestinal 
obstruction. It is also known to cause constipation due to the increased 
length of the colon. A redundant colon may not be able to explain your 
irritable bowel syndrome or the numbness of your leg.
