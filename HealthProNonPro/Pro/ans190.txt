Chronic urinary tract infections occur when the bacteria attach to the cell 
lining of the urinary tract. Often the new infection is caused by a 
bacteria different from the old infection. Treatment of chronic/recurrent 
urinary tract infections involves i) low dose 
trimethoprim-sulphamethoxazole (septran) or nitrofurantoin daily for 6 
months or longer ii) Short course of antibiotics when symptoms appear.Additions step that you can take to prevent recurrent infections are : 
drinking plenty of watre, taking showers instead of baths, wiping from from 
tto back, avoiding the use of douches and hygiene sprays and drinking 
cranberry juice.
