Most women do experience pain during intercourse during their lifetime. A 
common cause could be due the insufficient stimulation leading decreased 
lubrication in the vaginal tract. According to the history that you have 
given, the most probable diagnosis in your case would be secondary 
vaginismus due the traumatic events of abortion. In this condition there is 
involuntary contraction of the vaginal muscles during sex. The treatment is 
both physical and psychological. It may be helpful to talk to you partner 
and address each others concerns. Vaginal dilator may also be used. If the 
pain continues or gets worse, you may need to see your obgyn doctor for an 
internal examination. Medical conditions such as fibroids, pelvic 
inflammatory disease, urinary tract infection and trauma may all lead to 
pain during sex.
