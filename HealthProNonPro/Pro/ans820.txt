Lactose intolerance develops because of the inability of the intestines to 
digest lactose. The symptoms of lactose intolerance in infants are frothy 
stools, colic, fussy baby, irritable most of the time, vomiting, diaper 
rash.Lactose intolerance may also occur temporarily after an intestinal 
infection, parasitic infestation or food allergy. treatment involves 
exclusion of lactose from the diet. until symptoms resolve, usually at 
least 4 weeks. Dietetic advice should be sought to ensure nutritional 
adequacy of the diet. Lactose intolerance should be confirmed with a 
lactose challenge and the development of symptoms. You may need to talk to 
you doctor about giving your child lactose-free milk fortified with mineral 
and nutrients.
