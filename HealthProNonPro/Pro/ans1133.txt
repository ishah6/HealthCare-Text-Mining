Thank you for your question. Bilateral papilledema can be caused by either 
pseudotumor cerebri or a intracranial mass (tumor), or increased 
intracranial fluid (hydrocephalus). The only way to be certain of this is 
by conducting the MRI to visualize your brain and brain stem. We understand 
that this is distressing and you are anxious, but once the MRI results are 
report, your medical team will have a better idea as to what is causing 
your papilledema.Thank you for consulting AskTheDoctor.com
