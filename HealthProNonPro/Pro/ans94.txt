An ankle sprain is usually seen when there an inversion or twisting of the 
foot. Pain and inflammation of the joint is treated with rest, ice, 
compression bandages and elevation of the foot. If the pain is increasing, 
a plain radio-graph of the foot and ankle joint may be indicated to 
diagnose a fracture. Usually conservative therapy with analgesics, rest, 
ice and and ankle brace may be sufficient. More severe injury may require a 
below knee cast and surgery (third degree tears).
